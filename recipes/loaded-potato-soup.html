Loaded Potato Soup

Description

Loaded potato soup is hearty, creamy, and perfect for cold rainy days. I was first introduced to it by my late uncle, Silas, at a small local restaurant I'd never been to before. It quickly became my favorite soup and now I try to go at least once every week. I care about texture a lot, so for me what really sets it apart from other soups is the thick almost gravy-like consistency and soft chunks of potatoes. I don't want to understate just how important topping it with shredded cheese and crumbled bacon is though, it makes a world of difference for the presentation and adds some extra layers of flavor and texture that really send it over the top. In my opinion, loaded potato soup is a little plain, but with the cheddar and bacon you're able to make up for what it lacks. I hope you enjoy loaded potato soup as much as I do and share it with someone you think will enjoy it too!

Ingredients

  • 8 potatoes
  • 1 small white onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 8 cups of milk
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • shredded Cheddar cheese
  • raw bacon(I cook my bacon as part of my prep because using the bacon grease makes me feel less wasteful, but if you prefer bacon bits you'll need 1/2 cup of butter)
  • salt
  • pepper

Steps

Prep

  1. cube potatoes
  2. dice onion
  3. mince garlic
  4. shred cheese(you can ignore this step if you're using pre shredded cheddar)
  5. cook your bacon and be sure to save the grease(if you're using butter and bacon bits/precooked bacon you can ignore this step)
  6. give your bacon a rough chop once you've cooked it to your desired crispiness

Cooking

  1. set a large pot of water on the stove to boil, heat should be on high
  2. drop your potatoes in and set a timer for 12-15 minutes once the water starts boiling
  3. while the potatoes cook, add your bacon grease back to the pan(or melt your butter) and whisk in your flour until smooth
  4. once it's nice and smooth, slowly stir in the milk and onion
  5. bring it to a boil then reduce to medium heat and let it simmer
  6. strain and add your potatoes when the time goes off
  7. let it all cook another 5 or so minutes

Finishing Touches

  1. get yourself and whoever's with you a nice helping into bowls
  2. throw some cheese on top
  3. sprinkle some bacon on top
  4. salt and pepper to taste

Enjoy!
